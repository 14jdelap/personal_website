What I recommend

There's a character in Murakami's "Norwegian Wood" who only reads books by authors who have been dead for 30 years.

That’s the only kind of book I can trust, he says.

I try to follow this spirit in books and beyond.

Books

Non-fiction

  • I strongly recommend everything written by Robert Caro
  • "The Art of Learning" by Josh Waitzkins (it pairs well with Coursera's "Learning how to Learn")

Fiction

  • "La ciudad y los perros" ("The Hero of our Time") by Mario Vargas Llosa
  • "Cien años de soledad" ("Hundered Years of Solitude") by Gabriel García Márquez
  • Nicolai Gogol’s stories (start with "The Nose" or "Diary of a Madman")
  • Jerzy Kosiński's "Being There" and "The Painted Bird"

Movies

  • Almost everything from Alfred Hitchcock
  • "Mulholland Drive" by David Lynch
  • "Doctor Zhivago" by David Lean
  • "The Leopard" by Luchino Visconti
  • "Gone with the Wind" by Victor Fleming
  • "Blade Runner" by Ridley Scott
  • "Pulp Fiction" by Quentin Tarantino
  • "7 Samurai" and "Yojimbo" by Akira Kurosawa
  • "The Battle of Algiers" by Gillo Pontecorvo
  • "Barry Lyndon" and "The Shining" by Stanley Kubrick
  • "The Godfather" I and II by Francis Ford Coppola
  • "There Will be Blood" and "Boogie Nights" by David Lean
  • "Aguirre, Wrath of God", "Nosferatu", and "The Enigma of Kasper Hauser" by Werner Herzog
  • "Once Upon a Time in the West" by Sergio Leone
  • "Amour" by Michael Haneke
  • “Casablanca” by Michael Curtiz
